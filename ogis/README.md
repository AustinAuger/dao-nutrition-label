# Open Governance Integrity Standard (OGIS) v0.1

## Executive Overview

The Open Governance Integrity Standard (OGIS) is a voluntary transparency framework designed to enhance accountability and integrity within decentralized governance systems.

OGIS does not alter token-based voting rights or impose mandatory identity verification.  
Instead, it establishes structured governance reporting practices that disclose integrity indicators alongside traditional stake-weighted outcomes.

The objective of OGIS is to strengthen institutional confidence, mitigate governance manipulation risk, and preserve decentralization through transparency.

---

## Governance Risk Context

Decentralized governance systems face emerging risks, including:

- Concentrated capital dominance  
- Sybil attack vectors  
- Coordinated social sentiment manipulation  
- Narrative-driven volatility  
- Treasury exposure misalignment  
- Low participation opacity

OGIS addresses these risks through structured disclosure rather than enforcement mechanisms.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Voluntary Adoption**  
   Participation in OGIS reporting is optional.

2. **Capital Neutrality**  
   Token-weighted voting remains the base governance mechanism.

3. **Identity Optionality**  
   Identity verification systems may be integrated but are not required.

4. **Signal Transparency**  
   Sentiment and narrative metrics may be reported as contextual intelligence but must not secretly modify vote outcomes.

5. **Auditability**  
   All integrity metrics must be publicly defined and reproducible.

6. **Non-Override Architecture**  
   OGIS must not supersede core governance contracts unless explicitly codified by the DAO.

---

## OGIS Reporting Components

An OGIS-aligned governance report may include:

- Raw stake-weighted vote totals  
- Participation rate  
- Capital concentration metrics  
- Identity verification distribution (if applicable)  
- Sentiment integrity indicators (if integrated)  
- Advisory integrity-adjusted outcome (optional)  
- Treasury exposure classification

---

## Adoption Scope

OGIS is intended for:

- Token-governed DAOs  
- Institutional blockchain governance systems  
- Regulated digital asset networks  
- Hybrid on-chain/off-chain governance frameworks

## Status
